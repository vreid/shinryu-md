# Caspar Waldstein / Ryu Tae-sung

Born: March 29, 2001

Homeroom: 1年5組 (Class 5, Setagaya public high school)

Home base: Munich, Germany.

Appearance: 6'1", lean but broad-shouldered. Icy pale blue eyes. After the
exile: shoulders broadened by agricultural labor, hands roughened. A farm-built
body that reads as mysterious to teenagers.

Languages: German, Korean, English, Dutch, Japanese. He also downplays his
Japanese.

## Official Writing and Pronunciation

Germanic names ending in "-stein" are universally translated using the "sh-tine" pronunciation in Japanese, similar to historical figures like Einstein or the famous Beethoven piano sonata.

| Name Part | Katakana           | Romaji        | Pronunciation          |
| --------- | ------------------ | ------------- | ---------------------- |
| Caspar    | カスパー           | Kasupa        | Kah-soo-pah            |
| Waldstein | ヴァルトシュタイン | Varutoshutain | Vah-roo-toh-shoo-tie-n |

Due to the family-name-first rule, his name on formal documents, exams, and the attendance sheet appears as: ヴァルトシュタイン・カスパー (Varutoshutain Kasupa)

## Forms of Address

Because his family name contains seven Katakana characters, it is highly likely that everyone in the school will default to his first name for everyday conversation.

- **Teachers:** カスパーくん (Kasupa-kun) — Teachers use his relatively short first name attached to "-kun", the standard polite suffix used for male students.
- **Classmates:** キャス (Kyasu) — Friends bypass formal names altogether, using an anglicized/shortened nickname "Cas" (Kyasu) with no honorific suffix. This indicates a close, casual, and friendly relationship.
