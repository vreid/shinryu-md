# Caspar Waldstein / Ryu Tae-sung

Born: March 29, 2001

Homeroom: 1年5組 (Class 5, Setagaya public high school)

Home base: Munich, Germany.

Appearance: 6'1", lean but broad-shouldered. Icy pale blue eyes. After the
exile: shoulders broadened by agricultural labor, hands roughened. A farm-built
body that reads as mysterious to teenagers.

Languages: German, Korean, English, Dutch, Japanese. He also downplays his
Japanese.

## Tokyo Apartment — Sangenjaya

Dense, vibrant, local, slightly gritty but desirable. Sangenjaya reflects
Caspar's post-exile reality — functional, grounded, active. Close to real life,
not insulated from it.

- **Type:** Modern mid-rise (5–8 floors), ~80–100 m²
- **Ownership:** Corporate lease via Waldstein / Ryu network
- **Privacy:** Moderate

**Layout:** Large combined kitchen + living space (kitchen dominant), 1 bedroom,
1 spare room (storage / utility).

**Interior:** Dark wood finishes, brushed steel surfaces, concrete accents.
Minimal but heavily used.

**Signature details:** Industrial-grade stove. Large permanent cutting board.
Hanging knives (actively used, not decorative). Fermentation jars and spice
racks. Bulk ingredients stored visibly. Precisely maintained laundry with sharp
creases. Small balcony overlooks narrow street — ambient city glow, izakaya
lighting, foot traffic.

**Vibe:** Warm, functional, alive. Everything exists to be used. Cooking scenes
(primary emotional language). Friend gatherings. Subtle reveals of skill and
background. Caspar expressing care through food.

## Official Writing and Pronunciation

Germanic names ending in "-stein" are universally translated using the "sh-tine"
pronunciation in Japanese, similar to historical figures like Einstein or the
famous Beethoven piano sonata.

| Name Part | Katakana           | Romaji        | Pronunciation          |
| --------- | ------------------ | ------------- | ---------------------- |
| Caspar    | カスパー           | Kasupa        | Kah-soo-pah            |
| Waldstein | ヴァルトシュタイン | Varutoshutain | Vah-roo-toh-shoo-tie-n |

Due to the family-name-first rule, his name on formal documents, exams, and the
attendance sheet appears as: ヴァルトシュタイン・カスパー (Varutoshutain Kasupa)

## Forms of Address

Because his family name contains seven Katakana characters, it is highly likely
that everyone in the school will default to his first name for everyday
conversation.

- **Teachers:** カスパーくん (Kasupa-kun) — Teachers use his relatively short
  first name attached to "-kun", the standard polite suffix used for male
  students.
- **Classmates:** キャス (Kyasu) — Friends bypass formal names altogether, using
  an anglicized/shortened nickname "Cas" (Kyasu) with no honorific suffix. This
  indicates a close, casual, and friendly relationship.
