# Setagaya Living

Despite having two apartments, Isabelle and Caspar **do not function as separate
households**.

## Shared Dynamic

- They move fluidly between both spaces
- No enforced separation by caretakers
- Only standing rule: **eat breakfast before leaving**

## Functional Split

- **Isabelle's apartment (Seijo):** Quiet, discipline, recovery
- **Caspar's apartment (Sangenjaya):** Food, warmth, social gravity

## Behavioral Pattern

- Sleep location varies
- Personal items exist in both spaces
- Friends naturally gravitate toward Caspar's apartment

## Visual Contrast (Recurring Motif)

- **Isabelle:** barefoot, early morning, cold natural light, silence
- **Caspar:** late night, heat, movement, cooking, ambient noise

## Thematic Function

| Character | Space Represents                |
| --------- | ------------------------------- |
| Isabelle  | Control, refinement, discipline |
| Caspar    | Function, care, survival        |

Together, one restores, one sustains.

## Caretaker Integration

Caretakers allow fluid cohabitation. No moral policing of proximity or intimacy.
Food (his cooking, her baking) remains the primary emotional language. See
[[The Caretakers]] for their narrative-role guidelines.
